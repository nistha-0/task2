GAYA - A City of Historical Significance

Gaya, located in Bihar, India

,is a city with rich historical and cultural significance. It is renowned for being a major pilgrimage center,especially for Hindus and Buddhists.

Gaya is the second-largest city in Bihar surrounded by hills. It was described in the Hindu Epic of Ramayana and Mahabharta.

The city is home to the Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, marking the place where Gautam Buddha attained enlightenment.

The Phalgu River flows through Gaya, adding to the city's spiritual ambiance.

Vishnupad Temple is a sacred place on the banks of Phalgu river, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple features a 40-cm footprint purported to be of Vishnu incised into a block of basalt , known as Dharmasila which was retained when the deity stepped on Gayasura's chest before pinning him underground.

Gaya is the location at which Rama, with Sita and Lakshmana, offered pind-daan for his father, Dasharatha. Gaya has since remained a site of key importance for the performance of the pind-daan ritual.

Gaya is not only a place of religious importance but also a bustling urban center with a blend of ancient traditions and modern developments.

The city attracts tourists and devotees from all over the world,seeking spiritual solace and historical exploration.
